# ERM Tool Requirements Gap Assessment — ABC Holdings

*Prepared by the consolidating partner panel (10 functional assessors, 10 technical assessors, 10 consolidating partners). Assessed against the client requirement document + Protiviti AI additions + V2 roadmap. 58 line-items across 10 sections.*

**Coverage at a glance:** 9 fully in tool · 36 partial · 13 not yet built.
**Plus 30 additional features in the tool beyond the requirement** (see Section 4) — AI intelligence, financial quantification, governance framing, and trust/UX capabilities the client did not ask for.

---

## 1. Executive Summary

Across the 58 assessed requirements, the tool today **fully covers ~9 line-items, partially covers ~36, and does not yet address ~13**, indicating a platform that is conceptually complete and demo-ready in its core but still maturing toward production-grade enforcement and persistence. Its standout strengths are the **AI risk-intelligence layer** (approval-gated signal-to-risk correlation, emerging-risk suggestions, and a grounded read-only Copilot), the **what-if scenario workbench**, the **group and segment dashboards** (5x5 heatmaps, Portfolio Control Tower, ARC Pack and Executive Brief), and a credible **audit trail** spanning risk and mitigation CRUD. The core risk record itself — full Cause-Event-Impact structure with dual inherent/residual scoring and automatic banding — is the single most complete capability and needs no further work for the demo. The most important gaps are foundational rather than cosmetic: there is **no user-management or real RBAC enforcement** (personas are demo-mode and owners are free-text), **no historical snapshotting** (so period-over-period movement, trend reporting and data-freeze/versioning are unsupported), **no structured department tier**, and **no live data-source integration** for KRIs or project systems (P6/MS Project). For the **POC/MVP demo**, we recommend prioritizing the high-value, low-architecture wins — admin config surfaces, the approval/sign-off gate, evidence capture, control-to-risk many-to-many, KRI add/cadence, category and inherent-vs-residual dashboards, the in-context AI recommendations, and real document file ingestion — which collectively make the existing engine feel production-shaped. For **full implementation**, we recommend sequencing the structural builds in dependency order: identity/user management and RBAC first, then the historical snapshot store (which unblocks movement, trends, reporting cycles and narrative variance), followed by the integration workstreams (KRI data feeds, Capital Projects P6, regulatory obligation register) that depend on client-side system access. Net assessment: the tool is an exceptionally strong demonstration asset with an honest, well-bounded path to production — the heavy lifts are known, isolated, and properly deferrable.

## 3. Roadmap Rollup

### (a) Already in the tool — demo-ready today
- **Core risk record** — full Cause-Event-Impact structure with mandatory root causes, impacts and weighted drivers (§1.3, §2.2).
- **Dual scoring & auto-banding** — inherent (L×I) and residual (inherent × (1−control effectiveness)) with automatic Low/Medium/High/Critical rating (§2.3, §2.5).
- **Controls record** — 20 ICOFR-aligned controls with owner, frequency, status and deterministic effectiveness (§3.1).
- **KRI monitoring** — 8 fully-defined KRIs with appetite-anchored thresholds, traffic-light status, sparklines and breach history (§4.1).
- **AI signal-to-risk correlation** — approval-gated mapping of external signals to register risks, no auto-write (§P1.2).
- **AI emerging-risk suggestions** — accept/dismiss draft risk proposals (§P1.3).
- **AI Copilot** — grounded, read-only, drafts-only conversational Q&A over the register (§P3.3).

### (b) Add for the POC / MVP — high-value, low-architecture wins
- **Admin config surfaces** — editable categories, rating scales, hierarchy and KRI add-UI (§1.1, §1.9).
- **Approval / sign-off gate** — wire the existing workflow engine as a blocking maker-checker gate plus risk-acceptance-above-appetite records (§1.4, §7-8.1, §7-8.6).
- **Evidence capture & control-to-risk many-to-many** — file attachment on mitigations/controls and a control-to-risk junction (§1.8, §3.2, §3.3).
- **Dashboard quick wins** — category binning, inherent-vs-residual distribution, overdue-by-owner/function, recurring-breach analytics (§5.1, §5.3, §5.4).
- **AI enhancements** — in-context risk-drawer recommendations, control gap/duplication detection, per-risk scenario L/I overrides, scenario breach-list and rating-count deltas (§P1.4, §P1.5, §P1.6, §P3.1).
- **Framework-tag POC** — single-prompt ISO 31000/COSO/NIST benchmark tag (§P3.2).
- **Real document ingestion** — PDF/Word/PPT/Excel file parsing on the existing extraction workflow (§V2.4) plus the incident/loss-data model (§V2.2).
- **Scheduler + regional feeds** — cron polling and UAE regulatory/cyber connectors (§P1.1).

### (c) Defer to full implementation — structural builds, sequence by dependency
- **Identity & RBAC** (first) — user management, Risk Owner/Control Owner roles, enforced permissions, SSO (§1.2, §1.10, §1.12); unblocks ownership, delegation and department enforcement across the platform.
- **Historical snapshot store** (second) — time-series snapshotting that unblocks risk movement, period-over-period trends, reporting cut-off/freeze/versioning and narrative variance (§2.6, §2.8, §6.3, and the movement gaps in §6.2/§P5.4).
- **Configurable tolerance & escalation engine** — per-category/per-portfolio tolerance tiers with automated breach-to-escalation triggers (§5.2).
- **Treatment-to-control lifecycle** — promote completed mitigations into versioned controls with immutable history (§3.6).
- **Scenario persistence & comparison** — save/version scenarios and compare A vs B vs baseline (§P2.7).
- **Integration workstreams** (client-access dependent) — live KRI data feeds (§4.3), Capital Projects P6/MS Project earned-value module (§V2.1), and the structured regulatory obligation register with horizon scanning (§V2.3).
